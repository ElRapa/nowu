# Problem Statement: problem-002

## Core Problem

Significant decisions — architectural, strategic, business — are made and recorded, but the reasoning behind them, the alternatives considered, and the conditions at the time almost never survive. Months later, the human revisits a decision with no understanding of why it was made: they either repeat the full analysis from scratch (wasted effort) or treat the decision as an arbitrary constraint (drift). The absence of traceable reasoning means that past judgment has near-zero compounding value.

## Validated Personas

**Primary:** Raphael (the multi-project human) — makes consequential decisions across all domains (architecture, business, investment) and needs to be able to evaluate them in context months later.
**Secondary:** AI agents (nowu reviewer and implementer) — must detect when new work contradicts a prior decision and enforce the boundary before the change is merged.

## Confirmed Outcome Goals

1. Every significant decision — regardless of domain — carries its rationale, the alternatives that were rejected, and the conditions known at the time.
2. Any agent working on related problems can retrieve and enforce relevant decisions without human intervention.
3. When conditions change, the human can retrieve the decision, evaluate whether the original reasoning still holds, and either affirm or supersede it with confidence — rather than repeating the original analysis from scratch.
4. At every decision point, at least two distinct, well-reasoned options are generated and recorded — including the path not taken — before a choice is committed.

## Flagged Assumptions (resolved)

| Assumption | Resolution | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Decision capture will not create enough overhead to be abandoned | Accepted as a real risk. Mitigation: structured decision records must be generated by agents at decision points (NF-13), not manually authored by the human. The human reviews and approves, not writes. | Affects design: generation is automatic, not manual |
| The same decision format works for all domains (software ADR = business decision = investment thesis) | Partially accepted — the core fields (options, rationale, chosen, rejected, conditions) are domain-agnostic. Domain-specific templates (AP-06, RE-06) are thin wrappers over the same structure. | No major impact — confirmed by AP-06 and RE-06 as domain applications |

**Rationale:** Three interlocking capabilities must work together: generating options before a decision (NF-13), recording the chosen path with full rationale (NF-02), and enforcing decisions against new work (NF-02 enforcement path). These are distinct but interdependent workflow steps.

## Out of Scope (explicit)

1. Domain-specific decision templates for AP or RE — those are addressed in problem-008 (non-software dogfooding) which applies the decision capability to real domains.
2. Automatic detection of vision drift (NF-11 — that is v1.1 scope).
3. Knowledge conflict resolution across decision records (XP-04 — that is v1.1 scope).
4. Retrospective analysis of past decisions across multiple cycles (analytics / health metrics — v1.1 scope).

## Success Criteria

1. At any identified decision point, the system generates at minimum two distinct, well-reasoned options with explicit tradeoffs before the human makes a choice — and both the chosen path and the rejected alternatives are recorded.
2. Six months after a decision is recorded, the human can retrieve the decision and read: what was chosen, why, what was rejected, and what conditions existed at the time — without consulting git history, session logs, or external notes.
3. When an agent generates work that violates a recorded decision, the reviewer agent flags the specific violation with a reference to the original decision record — before the work reaches human review.

## Dependencies

None. This problem is independently solvable — it requires no cross-project infrastructure and no prior problem to be resolved first. problem-008 (non-software dogfooding) depends on this capability existing, but this problem does not depend on problem-008.
